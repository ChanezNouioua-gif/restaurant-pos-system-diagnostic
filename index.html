Risk #1 • CRITICAL Score: 15/100

Problem-Solution Fit: "Don't Realize Problem" = Fatal Killer

You indicated that restaurants "don't realize it's a problem". This contradicts your pain statement ("struggle with tracking"). If they don't realize it's a problem, they will never pay for your solution. Paper/pen is annoying but NOT critical - they can live with it. Without urgent pain, no sale.

Why this is risk #1

1.

"Annoying" vs "critical" pain: Paper = annoying but functional. Restaurant owners prefer saving 10,000 DA/month over paying for pretty software.

2.

Impossible sales cycle: Selling to someone who doesn't see the problem = educating the market. Takes 12-18 months and costs VERY high CAC.

3.

No validated pricing: You haven't tested what restaurants will actually pay. "Don't realize it's a problem" means they won't pay anything. Without customer interviews asking "what would you pay?", you're building blind.

Risk #2 • CRITICAL Score: 20/100

Broken Unit Economics: Subscription vs One-Time Purchase Battle

Traditional POS systems in Algeria = one-time purchase (30,000-80,000 DA). You want 5,000-10,000 DA/month subscription. After 3-8 months, you're already more expensive. After 1 year: 60,000-120,000 DA vs 30,000-80,000 DA one-time. After 3 years: 180,000-360,000 DA (3-5× more). Restaurant owners will calculate instantly: "Why pay forever when I can own it?" This pricing model kills sales in markets with one-time alternatives.

The math that kills your business

1.

Traditional POS (one-time): 50,000 DA paid once. Year 1: 50,000 DA. Year 3: 50,000 DA. Owned forever. Restaurant mentality: "I paid, I own, done."

2.

You (subscription 10,000 DA/month): Year 1: 120,000 DA (2.4× more). Year 3: 360,000 DA (7× more). Paying forever. Owner does the math in 10 seconds and says no.

3.

Subscription only works if: You provide continuous value they CAN'T get with one-time purchase (cloud sync, automatic updates, 24/7 support, integrations). "Modern UI" is one-time value, not ongoing. What justifies the monthly fee after month 1?

2.

B2B channels (what you need): Door-to-door sales, supplier partnerships, in-person demos, cold calling. Expensive and slow but the only way.

3.

Algerian market reality: Old-school mentality prefers paper over digital. They'll only pay for solutions that solve CRITICAL problems causing direct money loss. "Modern UI" won't convince them.

Why "modern UI" is not a moat

1.
